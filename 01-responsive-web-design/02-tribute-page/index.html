Nelson Mandela

The man who inspired change

Significant events of Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Nelson Mandela is born on July 18 in the small village of Mvezo in South Africa's Eastern Cape Province.
  • 1944 - Mandela joins the African National Congress (ANC), a political organization that would play a central role in the struggle against apartheid.
  • 1948 - The National Party comes to power in South Africa and begins implementing apartheid, a system of racial segregation and oppression.
  • 1960 - The Sharpeville Massacre occurs when police open fire on a peaceful protest, killing 69 people. The ANC is banned, leading to Mandela and other activists going underground.
  • 1962 - Mandela is arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for his role in organizing a workers' strike. This marks the beginning of his long imprisonment.
  • 1964 - Mandela and other ANC leaders are sentenced to life in prison during the Rivonia Trial for their involvement in planning sabotage against the apartheid government.
  • 1986 - International pressure against apartheid intensifies, with many countries imposing economic sanctions on South Africa.
  • 1990 - In a historic moment, South African President F.W. de Klerk announces the unbanning of the ANC, the South African Communist Party (SACP), and the Pan Africanist Congress (PAC). Mandela is released from prison on February 11.
  • 1993 - Mandela and de Klerk are jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to dismantle apartheid peacefully and establish multiracial elections.
  • 1994 - South Africa holds its first democratic elections in which all races can vote. Nelson Mandela becomes the country's first Black president, marking the end of apartheid and the beginning of a new era of reconciliation and hope.

"One of the most difficult things is not to change society, but to change yourself." - Nelson Mandela
